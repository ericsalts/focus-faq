The School District of Polk County recognizes the right of parents and guardians to be informed of their child's social, emotional, physical and intellectual development. Therefore, teachers will accurately communicate pupil growth and achievement through a reporting system of report cards, parent conferences and interim reports.

 QUESTIONS COMMONLY ASKED BY PARENTS:

Q. How do you decide what my child should learn in each grade?

A. The State of Florida holds all Florida schools accountable for teaching the skills outlined in a document called the Florida Standards. Polk County has developed guidelines for each grade level (K-12) based on these Florida Standards

Q. How do you decide whether to promote a child to the next grade level?

A. We believe students should master basic skills before moving to the next grade. A student is evaluated using many methods. Classroom tests, assignments, daily observations and standardized tests are some of the ways students are evaluated.

Q. What is the purpose of this report card?

A. It shows families how their child is mastering the knowledge and skills in each required subject. It also reports on study habits, attendance and behavior.

Q. How can I get more information about my child's progress?

A. When you send back the tear-off portion of the report card, be sure to check the box requesting a teacher conference. You also can call the school to request a conference any time during the year.
